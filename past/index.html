    Past SQAI Conferences

    Renowned Taiwan NCKU Professor Chi-Chuan Hwang explaining quantum Grover Algorithm applications in AI during SQAI 2021 to an audience of more than 100 participants.
    Since its founding, we have hosted innovative events that bring together researchers, students, and professionals at the cutting edge of quantum computing and artificial intelligence. Below is a summary of our past conferences and workshops - SQAI 2021 and SQAI 2023.

    SQAI 2021

    SQAI 2021 promotional poster
    May 20 & May 29, 2021
    15 speakers
    Multilingual & International
    250+ attendees
    Event Link / Day 1 Event Link / Day 2
    Overview: SQAI 2021 was our first large-scale event - a two-day virtual conference uniting voices from academia and industry. Presentations were conducted in both English and Mandarin, with speakers joining from around the world. The event included sessions on quantum neural networks, quantum AI feature transformation, AI ethics, quantum information theory, societal impacts of QAI, future trajectories of quantum and AI, and more.

    SQAI 2023

    May 7 – June 18, 2023
    100+ participants
    Original Event Website
    Overview: SQAI 2023 was a 7-week series of hands-on workshops and lectures focused on quantum computing fundamentals and applications. We explored everything from Qiskit to Grover's Algorithm, with participation from both newcomers and experienced practitioners. Each weekly session was conducted online, providing participants with both theoretical knowledge and practical experience.

    Workshop Series Schedule

    Week 1 (2023/5/7 21:30-22:30)
    Ace Est — Quantum Computing Applications and Introduction to Qiskit

    This talk explained the importance of quantum computing and its potential applications, introduced the Qiskit open source software and its main components, and outlined quantum computing concepts like qubits, superposition, and entanglement.

    Week 2 (2023/5/14)
    Quantum ML Workshop

    Participants explored the fundamentals of quantum machine learning and its application scenarios.

    Week 3 (2023/5/21 21:30-22:30)
    Ace Est — Quantum Mathematical Tools and Qiskit Implementation

    This talk introduced the mathematical tools of quantum computing, such as linear algebra, tensor products, and complex numbers, and their practical implementation in Qiskit.

    Week 4 (2023/5/28 21:30-22:30)
    Ace Est — Quantum Circuit Design Practice

    This talk introduced the principles of quantum circuit design and guided participants in creating their first quantum circuits - adders and entanglement circuits.

    Week 5 (2023/6/4 21:30-22:30)
    Ace Est — Building Your First Quantum Algorithm: Grover's Algorithm

    This talk introduced Grover's algorithm and its significance in quantum computing, explaining the key concepts behind the algorithm.

    Week 6 (2023/6/11 21:30-22:30)
    Mark Chen — Quantum Deep Learning: Challenges and Prospects

    Mark shared his experience in developing quantum deep neural networks to solve classical reinforcement learning problems, quantum programming frameworks, Qiskit and Microsoft Azure ML Studio.

    Week 7 (2023/6/18 21:30-22:30)
    Will Nien — How to Join IBM Quantum Awards Competition

    This session covered the motivation from Machine Learning to Quantum Computing, the contest topic of calculating lattice ground state, contest rules, results (Ansatz), and conclusions.
