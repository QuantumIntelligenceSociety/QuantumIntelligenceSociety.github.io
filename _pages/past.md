<div class="conference-container">
  <h2 class="conference-header">SQAI 2023</h2>
  
  <div class="conference-meta">
    <div class="conference-meta-item">
      <i class="fas fa-calendar"></i> May 7 – June 18, 2023
    </div>
    <div class="conference-meta-item">
      <i class="fas fa-users"></i> 100+ participants
    </div>
    <div class="conference-meta-item">
      <i class="fas fa-globe"></i> <a href="https://sqai2023.vercel.app/" target="_blank" style="color: #0366d6; text-decoration: none;">Original Event Website</a>
    </div>
  </div>
  
  <div class="conference-overview">
    <strong>Overview:</strong> SQAI 2023 was a 7-week series of hands-on workshops and lectures focused on quantum computing fundamentals and applications. We explored everything from Qiskit to Grover's Algorithm, with participation from both newcomers and experienced practitioners. Each weekly session was conducted online, providing participants with both theoretical knowledge and practical experience.
  </div>
  
  <h3>Workshop Series Schedule</h3>
  
  <div class="schedule-grid">
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 1 (2023/5/7 21:30-22:30)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ace.est1" target="_blank">Ace Est</a> — 
        <em>Quantum Computing Applications and Introduction to Qiskit</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">This talk explained the importance of quantum computing and its potential applications, introduced the Qiskit open source software and its main components, and outlined quantum computing concepts like qubits, superposition, and entanglement.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 2 (2023/5/14)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <em>Quantum ML Workshop</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">Participants explored the fundamentals of quantum machine learning and its application scenarios.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 3 (2023/5/21 21:30-22:30)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ace.est1" target="_blank">Ace Est</a> — 
        <em>Quantum Mathematical Tools and Qiskit Implementation</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">This talk introduced the mathematical tools of quantum computing, such as linear algebra, tensor products, and complex numbers, and their practical implementation in Qiskit.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 4 (2023/5/28 21:30-22:30)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ace.est1" target="_blank">Ace Est</a> — 
        <em>Quantum Circuit Design Practice</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">This talk introduced the principles of quantum circuit design and guided participants in creating their first quantum circuits - adders and entanglement circuits.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 5 (2023/6/4 21:30-22:30)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ace.est1" target="_blank">Ace Est</a> — 
        <em>Building Your First Quantum Algorithm: Grover's Algorithm</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">This talk introduced Grover's algorithm and its significance in quantum computing, explaining the key concepts behind the algorithm.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 6 (2023/6/11 21:30-22:30)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/mark.chen.sstm" target="_blank">Mark Chen</a> — 
        <em>Quantum Deep Learning: Challenges and Prospects</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">Mark shared his experience in developing quantum deep neural networks to solve classical reinforcement learning problems, quantum programming frameworks, Qiskit and Microsoft Azure ML Studio.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    
    <div class="schedule-row">
      <div class="schedule-date">Week 7 (2023/6/18 21:30-22:30)</div>
      <div class="schedule-content">
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/will.nien" target="_blank">Will Nien</a> — 
        <em>How to Join IBM Quantum Awards Competition</em>
        <p style="margin-top: 0.5rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">This session covered the motivation from Machine Learning to Quantum Computing, the contest topic of calculating lattice ground state, contest rules, results (Ansatz), and conclusions.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
